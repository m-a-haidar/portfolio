About Me

Welcome! I’m Muhammad Ali Haidar, a neuroscientist and biochemist with a proven track record of tackling complex scientific questions across diverse research fields. My work spans from investigating the neural mechanisms behind behavior and individuality to exploring innovative therapies for neurological conditions. I am driven by a passion for bridging molecular insights with real-world applications, contributing meaningful advances in our understanding of the brain.

Throughout my career, I’ve developed a wide-ranging skill set, allowing me to approach research problems from multiple perspectives. My adaptability and interdisciplinary expertise have been key in leading projects in basic neuroscience, advancing biochemistry studies, and responding to public health challenges. Whether in the lab or through public science communication, I strive to make impactful contributions and foster a collaborative environment.

I am always eager to connect with others who share a curiosity for science and technology. If you’re interested in discussing research, potential collaborations, or the latest trends in neuroscience and neurotechnology, let’s connect!

Research and Teaching Assitant - American University of Beirut
Oct 2020 - Feb 2022

My master’s thesis focused on investigating the neuroprotective effects of Mitoquinone (MitoQ) in a mouse model of traumatic brain injury (TBI). The study demonstrated that MitoQ improved neurological outcomes, reduced neuroinflammation, and enhanced cognitive and sensorimotor functions by targeting oxidative stress and inflammation pathways. Key findings included improved cognitive performance, reduced brain edema, and preserved neuronal integrity, highlighting MitoQ’s potential as a therapeutic for TBI.

As a Graduate Teaching Assistant for Cellular Metabolism and Protein Biochemistry, I supported lab sessions, assisted with grading, and developed teaching materials. I enhanced student engagement by creating detailed lab guides, streamlined grading with efficient feedback, and adapted to hybrid teaching during the COVID-19 pandemic, helping improve student outcomes and confidence.

Techniques and Skills:

  • Behavioral Assessments: Conducted comprehensive testing, including the Garcia neuroscore, grip strength, adhesive removal, object recognition, and Morris water maze tests, to assess motor, cognitive, and neurological functions.
  • Molecular Analysis: Utilized RT-qPCR for gene expression analysis, focusing on the Nrf2-ARE antioxidative pathway, and performed immunofluorescence staining to evaluate neuroinflammation and neuronal integrity.
  • Data Interpretation: Analyzed complex data sets to elucidate the relationship between oxidative stress modulation and improved neurological outcomes, contributing to a deeper understanding of TBI pathology.
  • Teaching and Course Development: Demonstrated complex concepts through interactive lab sessions. Contributed to the design of new experiments and updated teaching materials to reflect current research.
